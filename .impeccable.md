## Design Context

### Users

Edu-Canvas serves students in Grades 3–6 working independently at home, with optional parent support and asynchronous teacher monitoring. Teachers create and review activities, while school administrators manage policy and access. The core student job is to understand and complete a visual matching activity with minimal setup and clear next steps.

### Brand Personality

Calm, encouraging, quietly intelligent. The product should feel like a patient tutor: confidence-building and focused, never noisy or overstimulating. Its primary moment of delight is an educational “connection reveal” that makes the relationship between matched items clearer.

### Aesthetic Direction

Light-first with an optional dark theme. The current surface uses a warm paper-and-ink palette with a restrained blue accent, high contrast, and no neon gradients. Preserve that calm visual language as the product expands. The interface must adapt across phones, tablets, laptops, and desktops; support portrait and landscape; and handle touch, stylus, mouse, keyboard, English, Arabic, and RTL layouts.

### Design Principles

1. Put learning clarity before decoration: every visual flourish should help the student understand, choose, or recover.
2. Make the next action obvious for independent home use.
3. Keep interaction semantic and accessible; canvas and motion are enhancements, never the only path.
4. Reveal complexity progressively for teachers while preserving expert control.
5. Celebrate genuine understanding with brief, meaningful feedback rather than constant gamification.
